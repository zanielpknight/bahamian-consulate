Consular Services

Essential services for Bahamian nationals in Jamaica

Home › Consular Services

The Consulate General of The Bahamas in Kingston provides a range of consular services to Bahamian nationals residing in or visiting Jamaica. All services are available by appointment only.

The Consulate General processes passport applications and renewals on behalf of the Passport Office of The Bahamas. Whether you are applying for a new passport, renewing an expired or expiring travel document, or require an emergency travel certificate, our office is here to assist you through every step of the process.

Required Documents

  • Valid government-issued photo identification (e.g., Bahamian passport, driver's licence, or National Insurance card)
  • Completed passport application form (available for download below or at the Consulate)
  • Two (2) recent passport-sized photographs (2×2 inches, white background)
  • Current or most recently expired Bahamian passport
  • Applicable processing fee (see Fee Schedule below)

Processing Time

Standard passport applications are processed within 6–8 weeks from the date of submission. Processing times may vary depending on the volume of applications received by the Passport Office in Nassau.

Emergency Travel Documents: In urgent situations where a Bahamian national requires immediate travel—such as a medical emergency, death of a family member, or deportation—the Consulate can issue an Emergency Travel Document (ETD). ETDs are typically processed within 1–3 business days and are valid for a single journey to The Bahamas. Please contact the Consulate directly to initiate an emergency request.

The Consul General is authorized to administer oaths and perform notarial acts for Bahamian nationals and for documents intended for use within The Bahamas. Our notarial services include:

  • Affidavits and statutory declarations
  • Document authentication and certification
  • Certified true copies of original documents
  • Powers of attorney (general and special)
  • Witnessing of signatures

What to Bring

  • Valid government-issued photo identification
  • Original documents requiring notarization
  • Applicable fees (see Fee Schedule below)

Please note: Documents intended for use in The Bahamas may require additional authentication or legalization by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs in Nassau. The Consulate can advise on specific requirements based on the nature and purpose of your documents.

The welfare of Bahamian nationals in Jamaica is our highest priority. The Consulate General provides direct assistance and coordination for citizens who find themselves in difficult or emergency situations abroad. Our welfare services include:

  • Welfare checks on Bahamian nationals at the request of family members or the Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  • Hospital visits and liaison with Jamaican medical facilities
  • Emergency evacuation coordination during natural disasters and severe weather events
  • Assistance for Bahamian nationals who have been arrested or detained in Jamaica
  • Death notifications and repatriation of remains to The Bahamas
  • Assistance for victims of crime, including liaison with Jamaican law enforcement

Emergency Contact

For urgent consular assistance, contact the Consulate via WhatsApp at +1 (876) 436-8768. This line is monitored outside of regular office hours for genuine emergencies involving the safety or welfare of Bahamian nationals.

Hurricane & Natural Disaster Response

The Consulate has a proven track record in emergency response. During Hurricane Beryl (July 2024), the team coordinated the emergency airlift of 35 Bahamian nationals from Jamaica. In Hurricane Melissa (October 2025), the Consulate again led evacuation and welfare operations, ensuring the safety of all registered Bahamian nationals on the island. We maintain active emergency protocols and encourage all Bahamians in Jamaica to register with the Consulate.

Please note: Jamaican nationals do not require a visa to enter The Bahamas for stays of up to three (3) months for tourism or business purposes. A valid Jamaican passport is required for entry.

The Consulate General provides guidance and information on Bahamian visa and immigration matters, including:

  • Visa requirement guidance for nationals of countries other than Jamaica
  • General immigration queries related to travel to or residence in The Bahamas
  • Referrals to the Department of Immigration, Government of The Bahamas, for work permits, permanent residency, and spousal permit applications
  • Information on CARICOM Skilled Nationals provisions

For comprehensive visa and travel information, please visit our Visas & Travel page.

Beyond direct citizen services, the Consulate General represents the interests of the Commonwealth of The Bahamas in Jamaica and at international organizations headquartered in Kingston. Our diplomatic functions include:

  • Representation at the International Seabed Authority (ISA), where the Consul General serves as The Bahamas' first Permanent Representative
  • Participation in CARICOM regional meetings and consultations held in Jamaica
  • Engagement with the Group of Latin American and Caribbean Countries (GRULAC) at international forums
  • Processing of diplomatic notes and official correspondence between The Bahamas and Jamaica
  • Liaison with Jamaican government ministries on bilateral matters, including trade, security cooperation, and mutual legal assistance
  • Protocol support for visiting Bahamian government officials and delegations

The Consulate General is committed to supporting the Bahamian community in Jamaica and fostering cultural ties between our two nations. Our programs include:

Student Support

Approximately 76 Bahamian students are currently enrolled at the University of the West Indies, Mona campus. The Consulate provides ongoing support including orientation assistance, welfare checks, liaison with university administration, and emergency support. We also facilitate communication between students and the Ministry of Education in Nassau regarding scholarship matters.

Majority Rule Day Celebrations

Each January, the Consulate hosts its flagship cultural event commemorating Majority Rule Day—a defining moment in Bahamian history. The inaugural lecture series, held in January 2026, brought together members of the Bahamian diaspora, Jamaican officials, and the diplomatic community for an evening of reflection and celebration.

Cultural Lecture Series

The Consulate hosts periodic lectures and discussions on topics related to Bahamian history, culture, governance, and the bilateral relationship between The Bahamas and Jamaica. These events are open to the Bahamian community and the wider public.

Diaspora Engagement

We maintain regular contact with the Bahamian diaspora in Jamaica through community gatherings, holiday celebrations, and informational sessions. If you are a Bahamian national living in Jamaica and have not yet registered with the Consulate, we encourage you to do so by contacting our office.
