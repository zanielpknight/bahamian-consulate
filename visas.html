Visa & Travel Information

Entry requirements and travel guidance for The Bahamas and Jamaica

Home › Visa & Travel

No Visa Required

Jamaican nationals may visit The Commonwealth of The Bahamas for up to three (3) months without a visa. Bahamian nationals may visit Jamaica for up to six (6) months without a visa.

For Jamaican Nationals

Entry Requirements

  • Valid passport with at least six (6) months remaining validity from date of entry
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel reservation, invitation letter, or rental confirmation)
  • Proof of sufficient funds to cover the duration of stay
  • Completed immigration card (provided on arrival)

Customs & Declarations

Visitors to The Bahamas are permitted to bring in personal effects and reasonable quantities of goods duty-free. Customs allowances include up to 200 cigarettes or 50 cigars, one quart of spirits, and personal items for the duration of stay. All visitors must complete a customs declaration form upon arrival. Prohibited items include illegal drugs, firearms and ammunition (without prior written approval), and certain agricultural products. Failure to declare restricted items may result in fines, confiscation, or prosecution under Bahamian law.

Health Requirements

There are no mandatory vaccination requirements for travellers entering The Bahamas from Jamaica. However, travellers are advised to ensure routine vaccinations are up to date, including measles-mumps-rubella (MMR), diphtheria-tetanus-pertussis, and COVID-19 boosters. Visitors travelling from countries with known yellow fever risk may be required to present proof of yellow fever vaccination. Travel health insurance is strongly recommended for all visitors.

Travelling with Children

Minors (under 18) travelling to The Bahamas without both parents must carry a notarized letter of consent from the absent parent(s) or legal guardian, along with a copy of the parent's or guardian's government-issued identification. If the minor is travelling with a non-parent adult, a notarized authorization from both parents is required. Immigration officers may request these documents at the port of entry, and failure to produce them may result in denial of entry.

For Bahamian Nationals

Entry into Jamaica

Bahamian nationals may enter Jamaica for up to six (6) months without a visa for tourism, business, or personal purposes. A valid Bahamian passport is required for entry, and immigration officers may request proof of return or onward travel, proof of accommodation, and evidence of sufficient financial means. Visitors intending to work or study in Jamaica must obtain the appropriate permit or authorization from Jamaican immigration authorities prior to or upon arrival.

Consular Registration

Bahamian nationals residing in or visiting Jamaica for extended periods are encouraged to register with the Consulate General for welfare tracking and emergency communication.

To register, please contact the Consulate at:

  • WhatsApp: +1 (876) 436-8768
  • Email: Alvetaknight@bahamas.gov.bs
  • In person: 2-4 Gladstone Drive, Kingston (by appointment)

Returning to The Bahamas

Bahamian nationals returning home from Jamaica must complete a customs declaration form upon arrival in The Bahamas. Personal goods and gifts up to a combined value of $300 BSD may be imported duty-free. Items exceeding this threshold are subject to applicable customs duties. Travellers are reminded that certain agricultural products, plants, and animal products may be restricted or prohibited from entry into The Bahamas. For a complete list of restricted items, consult the Bahamas Customs, Excise & Immigration Department before travel.
